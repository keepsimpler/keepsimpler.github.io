##[Rational Function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rational_function) In mathematics, a rational function is any polynomial divided by a polynomial, i.e. both the numerator and denominator are polynomials. A particular rational function is: $f(x) = \frac{ax+c}{bx+d}$ (1) whose numerator and denominator are both first-order polynomials, i.e. linear functions. Equation (1) is nearly linear for small values of $x$ but reaches a constant value as $x$ becomes large (an "asymptote"). ###Reference [A Biologist's Guide to Mathematical Modelling in Ecology and Evolution](http://press.princeton.edu/titles/8458.html)

Rational Function

In mathematics, a rational function is any polynomial divided by a polynomial, i.e. both the numerator and denominator are polynomials.

A particular rational function is:

f(x)=ax+cbx+d (1)

whose numerator and denominator are both first-order polynomials, i.e. linear functions.

Equation (1) is nearly linear for small values of x but reaches a constant value as x becomes large (an "asymptote").
